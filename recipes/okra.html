Okra Recipe

What is Okra

Okra is a common relish in Africa and it's featured in numerous international recipes. It's a fruit that's eaten like a vegetable and delicious when prepared properly. It's widely available both fresh and frozen.

Okra can be cooked in many ways. When stewed or cooked with liquid, okra can get slimy, but there are tricks to minimize that aspect. Quick-cooking and dry heat create crunchy okra with a crisp, grassy flavor.

Ingredients

The following are the ingredients needed for Okra soup;

  • 500g okra
  • 1 medium yellow or red onion
  • 2 medium tomatoes
  • 3 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne, optional

Steps to Make It

  1. Gather the ingredients



  2. Trim off and discard the stem ends from the okra and cut the pods into 1/4- to 1/2-inch slices.
    • NB; Set the prepped okra aside as seen below;



  3. Chop the tomatoes, reserving their juices; peel and thinly slice the onion; set them both aside.



  4. Heat the oil in a large frying pan or sauté pan over high heat. Once the oil is hot,

    add the chopped onions and the tomatoes, any juices they've released, the salt and 1/2 cup of water.




  5. Stir to combine everything and then cover. Reduce the heat to maintain a steady simmer. Until the Okra is tender.


  6. Serve the okra hot or warm.
